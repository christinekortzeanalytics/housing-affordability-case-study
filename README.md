Housing Affordability Case Study

This project analyzes the relationship between wages and rent in Sussex County, Delaware and Philadelphia, Pennsylvania using 2024 data from the U.S. Census Bureau and the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Key Questions

  *How do wages compare between the two regions?

  *How does rent compare?

  *How many hours of work are needed to afford housing?

  *How do seasonal vs. year‑round economies affect affordability?

Methods

  *Converted weekly wages to hourly wages

  *Calculated hours needed to afford median rent

  *Compared wage–rent gaps across regions

  *Interpreted results using economic context

Files

  *housing_affordability_case_study.pdf — Full written analysis

  *sussex_2024.csv — Wage and rent data for Sussex County

  *philadelphia_2024.csv — Wage and rent data for Philadelphia

Summary

Philadelphia has higher rent but significantly higher wages, resulting in fewer hours needed to afford housing. Sussex County’s lower wages and seasonal labor market widen the wage–rent gap, reducing affordability despite lower rent levels.
